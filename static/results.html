Future impact of research may be present but not clearly stated or lacking. Importance of research poorly stated or lacking. PurposeFundamental question is clearly stated and relevant. Hypothesis is a testable statement that directly relates to the fundamental question. Range of expected outcomes are expressed quantitatively and include proper statistics that would support hypothesis.Fundamental question and hypothesis are adequately stated. Hypothesis is a testable statement. Range of expected outcomes are quantitative, and include some statistical component, which demonstrate support for the hypothesis.Fundamental question and hypothesis are stated but loosely tied. Hypothesis is not testable or relevant. Expected outcomes are not expressed quantitatively, and lack adequate statistics, nor demonstrate support for the hypothesis.No research question(s), hypothesis, or expected outcomes provided, no effort made, or deemed unacceptable.                                          
Procedures (x2)Step-by-step description of experiment in outline form. Procedure is sequential, self-sufficient and easily reproducible. Variables (independent/dependent), control, experimental/control groups, and parameters are clearly explained within the design. Nature of samples and sizes are explained. Description of chemical/biological agents used in the steps are quantitative. Method of data collection is clear and includes techniques and instruments needed. Proper citations of references included for more complicated procedures.Steps are present, and in outline form, but lack some necessary detail. Procedure is sequential, but could be made clearer to be more easily followed. Variables (independent/dependent), control, experimental/control groups, and parameters are present but not abundantly clear within the design. Nature of samples and sizes are mentioned, but need more detail. Description of chemical/biological agents used in the steps are not adequately quantitative. Method of data collection including techniques and instruments needed not fully clear. Citations of references for more complicated procedures are incomplete.Steps are not adequately listed in outline form, and lack any detail. Procedure is non-sequential, and cannot be followed. Variables (independent/dependent), control, experimental/control groups, and parameters are mostly missing or not made clear within the design. Nature of samples and sizes are not described in any detail. Description of chemical/biological agents used in the steps are lacking and not quantitative. Method of data collection is severely lacking. Citations of references for more complicated procedures are lacking.No experimental methods provided, no effort made, or deemed unacceptable.x2                                        
Risk and SafetyClear summary of safety risks and precautions necessary for carrying out research plan. PPE necessary to implement safety measures are stated. Assessment includes proper BSL designation and reason. Proper handling, use and storage of high risk materials in the lab is delineated. Adequate summary of safety risks and precautions necessary for carrying out research plan, but somewhat incomplete. PPE necessary to implement safety measures are incomplete. Assessment includes proper BSL designation and reason is unclear or miscategorized. Proper handling, use and storage of high risk materials in the lab is mentioned, though somewhat incomplete. Poor summary of safety risks and precautions necessary for carrying out research plan, and is largely incomplete and lacking. PPE necessary to implement safety measures are largely neglected. Assessment lacks proper BSL designation. Proper handling, use and storage of high risk materials in the lab is neglected. No mention of potential risks and safety measures required in experimentation, no effort made, or deemed unacceptable.                                          
Data AnalysisDescription of how the data will be quantitatively and statistically treated post-collection is lucid and adequate. Clearly defines how the data will be compared quantitatively between the experimental and control groups. Describes in adequate detail at least one statistical test(s) that will be run on the data to determine confidence in desired relationship(s) between the data.Section describes quantitative and statistical treatment of post-collection data, though may be missing some key elements including how the data will be compared quantitatively between the experimental and control groups. Includes what statistical test(s) will be run on the data to determine confidence in desired relationship(s) between the data, though lacks in sufficient detail.Section largely fails to describe any quantitative and statistical treatment of post-collection data, including how the data will be compared quantitatively between the experimental and control groups. Lacks any description of a statistical test to be run on the data to determine confidence in desired relationship(s) between the data.No mention of a means of post-collection data analysis, no effort made, or deemed unacceptable.                                          
BibliographyBibliography listed in alphabetical order by author last name. Includes at least 10 reliable sources, which dominantly consist of research and review articles. Web resources are properly referenced, and from reliable sources/agencies. References match the citations in your research plan. There are no roaming references; all references are cited within the text of your research plan.Bibliography listed, though alphabetical order by author last name may be inaccurate. Includes less than 10 reliable sources, few of which consist of research and review articles, and rely mostly on web resources that are not all from reliable sources/agencies. References match the citations in your research plan. Few roaming references that may not be cited within the text of your research plan.Bibliography incomplete, and in no apparent order. Includes less than 6 reliable sources, none of which consist of any research and review articles, and rely mostly on web resources that are not from reliable sources/agencies. References don't match the citations in your research plan, or are not cited within the text of your research plan.References of literature not provided, no effort made, or deemed unacceptable.
